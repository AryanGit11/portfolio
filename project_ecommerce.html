Cohort Retention & RFM Segmentation

The Problem

Why aren't customers coming back?

The business had growing transaction volumes but couldn't explain why revenue felt stagnant. No visibility into which customers were churning, which were worth saving, and which had already gone silent. Without answers, every marketing decision was a guess.

Methodology

How the analysis was structured

1
Data Consolidation & Cleaning Merged two Excel sheets (1,067,371 rows) into a single DataFrame. Removed nulls, cancellations, negative quantities, and duplicates, resulting in 779,425 clean rows.
2
Feature Engineering Derived Total Price, Cohort Month, and Cohort Index columns to enable time-based retention tracking.
3
Cohort Retention Analysis Built a 25-month cohort heatmap showing what percentage of each acquisition cohort returned in subsequent months.
4
RFM Segmentation Scored all 5,878 customers on Recency, Frequency, and Monetary value. Grouped into 7 actionable segments: Champions, Loyal, At-Risk, Lost, and more.
5
SQL Deep-Dive Ran 4 targeted SQL queries using aggregations, JOINs, CTEs, and window functions to validate Python findings and surface additional patterns.
6
Power BI Dashboard Built a 3-page interactive dashboard for stakeholders: retention heatmap, RFM segment breakdown, and revenue by geography.
Key Findings

What the data revealed

50%+

Of customers never make a second purchase. First-to-second purchase conversion is the single biggest revenue leak in the business.

1,551

At-Risk customers with the highest average revenue (£6,271), the most valuable and most urgent segment to re-engage.

82%

Of total revenue comes from the UK alone, revealing heavy geographic concentration and untapped potential in other markets.

Dec

Cohorts show anomalous 12-month retention spikes, suggesting seasonal buyers who return annually rather than becoming regular customers.

Recommendations

What the business should do next

Fix first-purchase retention first. More than half of customers leave after one order. A targeted second-purchase campaign (discount or follow-up email within 30 days) would have the highest ROI of any initiative.
Re-engage the At-Risk segment immediately. 1,551 customers averaging £6,271 revenue are showing early churn signals. Personalized win-back outreach before they go cold is critical.
Treat December as a separate customer cohort. Seasonal buyers behave differently; measure them on annual retention, not monthly, and design campaigns accordingly.
Diversify geographic revenue. 82% UK concentration is a business risk. International growth campaigns targeted at the next-largest markets should be a strategic priority.
