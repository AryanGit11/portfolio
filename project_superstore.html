Superstore Sales &
Profitability Analysis

A retail superstore had healthy sales figures but was bleeding money in specific categories. This analysis pinpointed exactly where losses were happening and why, and what to do about it.

The Problem

Strong sales. Hidden losses.

Overall revenue looked healthy year on year, but profit margins told a different story. The business had no clear view of which product categories, regions, or customer segments were actually profitable vs. which were quietly destroying value through aggressive discounting and poor margin management.

Methodology

How the analysis was structured

1
Data Understanding & Cleaning Explored the 9,994-row dataset across 21 columns. Validated data types, checked for nulls and duplicates, confirmed date ranges and category structure.
2
Feature Engineering Derived profit margin percentages, discount impact scores, and year/quarter/month time columns to enable trend and seasonality analysis.
3
KPI Analysis Computed headline KPIs — total sales, total profit, profit margin, segmented by category, sub-category, region, and customer segment.
4
Root-Cause Analysis Drilled into loss-making sub-categories to isolate whether losses were driven by pricing, discounting, or category mix. Tables emerged as the primary culprit.
5
Regional & Segment Analysis Cross-tabulated losses by region and customer segment to identify geographic concentration of the discount problem; the East region was disproportionately impacted.
6
Time-Based Analysis & Dashboard Identified strong November–December seasonality. Built an interactive Power BI dashboard covering profitability by category, region trend lines, and discount impact visualization.
Key Findings

What the data revealed

-$17.7K

Tables sub-category generated the largest absolute loss, the single biggest drag on company profitability across all product lines.

~26%

Average discount rate was identified as the primary loss driver. Discounts were being applied indiscriminately, destroying margins even on high-volume products.

East

The East region accounted for -$11,025 in Tables-related losses; the discount problem was geographically concentrated, not a company-wide pattern.

$1.16M

Consumer segment drove the highest sales ($1.16M, $134K profit), but Corporate and Home Office segments had better margin efficiency per dollar of revenue.

Recommendations

What the business should do next

Cap discounts on Tables immediately. The ~26% average discount is directly causing the -$17.7K loss. A hard cap at 10–15% for this sub-category would recover margin without meaningfully impacting volume.
Audit the East region's discount approval process. -$11,025 of Tables losses are concentrated here. Regional sales teams may be using discounts to hit volume targets at the expense of profit.
Prioritise Corporate and Home Office segments. Despite lower sales volume, their margin efficiency exceeds the Consumer segment. Targeted account growth here is more profitable per deal.
Plan inventory and promotions around November–December seasonality. Clear demand spikes in Q4; capitalise with margin-preserving campaigns rather than blanket discounts.
